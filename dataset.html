SC-8000 is a longitudinal clinical imaging dataset comprising over 8,000 anonymized photographs of basal cell carcinoma (BCC) and squamous cell carcinoma (SCC) treatment sites. Images are captured at baseline (prior to biopsy and treatment) and at regular two-week intervals throughout a standardized course of 30 applications of the Shen Cocktail, a combination topical immunotherapy.

Collected in routine clinical practice over nearly two decades (2008-present), SC-8000 represents one of the largest single-therapy, longitudinal dermatology image datasets to date. The dataset captures the full progression of immune response and healing over time, enabling analysis of visual biomarkers such as inflammation, tissue breakdown, and reconstruction. SC-8000 is purpose-built for computer vision projects, and serves as the foundational dataset for DermLens, a system to monitor visual biomarkers during topical immunotherapy in real-world clinical settings.

Overview

The dataset includes multiple acquisition devices, longitudinal follow-ups, and clinician notes for contextual diagnosis. We prioritize balanced representation across age, sex, and skin tone.

  • Images: 10,000 clinical photos
  • Labels: 14 primary diagnostic categories, 62 subtypes
  • Splits: Patient-level train/val/test with temporal holdout
