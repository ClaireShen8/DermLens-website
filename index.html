DermLens is a dermatology-focused research initiative building trustworthy, clinically useful computer vision tools. We study lesion segmentation, multimodal diagnosis, and explainability with a focus on real-world deployment constraints. Our work bridges data curation, model design, and human-centered evaluation to support earlier detection and improved care.

Highlights

  • June 2024
    DermLens v2 baseline released with calibrated uncertainty and triage-ready risk scores.
  • May 2024
    Expanded dataset with diverse skin tones and biopsy-confirmed ground truth.
  • April 2024
    Human factors study on clinician trust and interpretability published.

Research Focus

We emphasize dataset transparency, rigorous clinical validation, and explainable model outputs. Current efforts include temporal lesion tracking, risk-aware screening, and fairness auditing.
