DermLens is a computer vision research project of Shen Dermatology (Temecula, California) focused on developing quantitative tools for monitoring treatment response during non-surgical skin cancer therapy.

The foundation of this work is SC-8000, a longitudinal clinical imaging dataset comprising over 8,000 photographs collected between 2008 and the present. The dataset documents patients before and throughout treatment with the Shen Cocktail, a combination topical immunotherapy for basal cell carcinoma (BCC) and squamous cell carcinoma (SCC). Images are captured at baseline and at regular intervals during a typical six-week treatment course.

Unlike many dermatology datasets designed for lesion diagnosis, SC-8000 is purpose-built for treatment monitoring. All images represent a single therapeutic protocol administered under a consistent clinical framework, enabling detailed study of longitudinal visual biomarkers, particularly erythema (treatment-induced redness) and its progression over time.

The goal of DermLens is to develop interpretable, clinically meaningful computer vision methods that quantify changes in erythema and related visual features during treatment. Rather than classifying lesions, our work focuses on measuring within-patient change over time and integrating image-derived signals with patient-reported symptoms to support clinical decision-making during follow-up.

We believe SC-8000 is unique in its scale, duration, and longitudinal consistency, representing more than 15 years of real-world clinical practice using a single non-surgical therapy. DermLens welcomes collaboration with medical image computing and machine learning researchers interested in longitudinal analysis, color-based biomarkers, and clinically grounded computer vision methods for treatment monitoring.
